 Pre-diabetes Risk   

What does pre-diabetes mean? Prediabetes means you have a higher than normal blood sugar level. It's not high enough to be considered type 2 diabetes yet, but with some simple changes, adults and children with prediabetes can prevent type 2 diabetes.

Key Findings from the 2020 National Diabetes Statistics Report include:
  • 34.2 million Americans—just over 1 in 10—have diabetes.
  • 88 million American adults—approximately 1 in 3—have prediabetes.
What does diabetes do to your body?
High Blood sugar damages your body silently for many years before you “feel” symptoms.
High Blood sugar damages nerves, blood vessels, and kidneys.
Diabetes is the major cause of blindness, kidney failure and neuropathy (constant pain, numbness, tingling and burning sensation in hands and feet), as well as pain and suffering related to poor health.
